The Bends

Original Screenplay — Drama, Single Scene
INT. RECORDING STUDIO – EVENING A sleek, modern studio. LED lights glow blue over expensive audio equipment. GRANT, STEVEN, and MADISON lounge on the couch and chairs, drinks in hand, half scrolling on phones. GRANT (sarcastic) He's late. (pause) Shocker. STEVEN Watch him show up with some SoundCloud nonsense and a half baked sob story. MADISON I bet it's more acoustic sadness about how no one understands him. GRANT (mocking) “I made this track in my mom's basement after my cat died.” They all laugh. STEVEN He's just gonna be like the rest (pause) forgettable. MADISON Honestly, we're wasting time. We could be out partying or something. Suddenly, the studio door opens. SANTIAGO (early 20s, modest clothes, headphones around his neck) walks in, calm and focused. SANTIAGO Hey everyone. Thanks for having me. GRANT, STEVEN, and MADISON glance up coldly. No one responds. Santiago doesn't seem bothered. He nods, walks over to the console, and begins unpacking a small USB drive and a notebook. GRANT Let's make this quick, we have places to be, you know. SANTIAGO (CONT'D) Got the track ready. It's called “Jigsaw Falling into Place.” He plugs in, taps a few buttons. The room fills with silence, then — MUSIC BEGINS TO PLAY. A pulsing acoustic guitar riff builds, urgent and hypnotic. The beat kicks in subtle at first, then tense and propulsive. Santiago's haunting vocals cut through: vulnerable, assured, and mesmerizing. Grant sits up. Steven lowers his phone. Madison leans forward. GRANT (quiet) Wait... what? The track tightens like a noose. Layers of rhythm and melody stack with eerie precision. Santiago's voice swells in intensity — controlled chaos, emotional but never out of control. STEVEN (awed) Is this... him? MADISON It's beautiful. Dark, but... electric. The chorus explodes: “You've got a light, you can feel it on your back...” The track drives forward, each verse like a heartbeat syncing with theirs. GRANT This is insane. Who... who is this guy? Madison is seen wiping tears off her face. Steven has a look that makes him seem hundreds of miles away. The song ends in a tense, unresolved fade. A beat of silence. Then — STEVEN How did you make that? Like, where did that sound come from? MADISON Did you produce this all yourself? Seriously, that was (long pause) amazing... 2. GRANT (genuinely) Dude, that was better than half our signed artists. Santiago calmly unplugs the USB. SANTIAGO Appreciate the feedback. Can I speak to your boss? He slings his backpack over his shoulder. SANTIAGO (CONT'D) I'd like to talk contract. Reach out if you are interested in more.
