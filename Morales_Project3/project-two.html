Screenplay

The Bends

Project Overview

The Bends is an original screenplay set in a sleek recording studio late one evening. Three industry insiders wait impatiently for an unknown artist to arrive. When Santiago walks in calm and focused, they dismiss him immediately. Then he plays his track, and everything changes.

Script Excerpt

INT. RECORDING STUDIO - EVENING A sleek, modern studio. LED lights glow blue over expensive audio equipment. GRANT, STEVEN, and MADISON lounge on the couch and chairs, drinks in hand, half scrolling on phones. GRANT (sarcastic) He's late. (pause) Shocker. STEVEN Watch him show up with some SoundCloud nonsense and a half baked sob story. MADISON I bet it's more acoustic sadness about how no one understands him. The studio door opens. SANTIAGO (early 20s, modest clothes, headphones around his neck) walks in, calm and focused. SANTIAGO Hey everyone. Thanks for having me. He nods, walks to the console, and begins unpacking a small USB drive and a notebook. Grant, Steven, and Madison watch coldly. Santiago doesn't seem bothered. SANTIAGO (CONT'D) Got the track ready. It's called "Jigsaw Falling into Place." MUSIC BEGINS TO PLAY. A pulsing acoustic guitar riff builds, urgent and hypnotic. Santiago's haunting vocals cut through: vulnerable, assured, and mesmerizing. Grant sits up. Steven lowers his phone. Madison leans forward. GRANT (quiet) Wait... what? The song ends in a tense, unresolved fade. A beat of silence. GRANT (genuinely) Dude, that was better than half our signed artists. Santiago calmly unplugs the USB. SANTIAGO Appreciate the feedback. Can I speak to your boss? He slings his backpack over his shoulder. SANTIAGO (CONT'D) I'd like to talk contract.

The Challenge

The challenge was writing a script where the power shifts entirely through subtext and performance rather than action. The three industry characters needed to feel genuinely threatening at first so that Santiago's quiet confidence would land as something unexpected and earned.

My Role

I was the sole writer. I developed the concept, wrote multiple drafts, and focused heavily on making the dialogue feel naturalistic without losing its rhythm. The goal was a script that reads quickly but hits hard.

What I Learned

This project showed me how much a single scene can accomplish when every line is doing double duty. The dialogue carries character, conflict, and theme all at once. I also learned how silence and pauses on the page communicate as much as words do.
